Clinical Research Analytics

Verified Dataset: 2026

I. Demographic Metrics (1-5)

1. Age Distribution

A predominantly youth-focused sample, with 65.1% of respondents in the 18-22 age bracket.

2. Gender Identification

Female participants represent the majority (65.1%) of the survey sample.

3. Faculty / Field

High representation from medical fields (79.1%), ensuring data quality.

4. Weight Distribution (kg)

Weight metrics concentration is observed within the 70-80kg range.

5. Height Distribution (cm)

Height averages peak between 165cm and 175cm across the group.

II. Lifestyle & Diet Habits (6-11)

6. Healthy Diet Adherence

Only 7% strictly follow a healthy diet, with many adhering "sometimes".

7. Fast Food Frequency

25.6% consume fast food weekly, but monthly consumption is most common.

8. Fried Food Preference

A strong preference for fried foods is clear, with only 11.6% abstaining.

9. Soft Drink Consumption

25.6% report daily soft drink intake, a significant risk factor.

10. Daily Water Hydration

1-2L daily is the average, but 18.6% consume less than 1L.

11. Fruit & Veg Servings

Most eat at least one serving, but only 25.6% eat 2-3 servings daily.

III. Symptom Analysis (12-15)

12. Colon Symptom Frequency

Chronic discomfort is reported by 23.3%, with 34.9% having occasional issues.

13. Primary Symptoms Logged

Abdominal pain (39.5%) and Bloating (37.2%) are the top complaints.

14. Spicy Food Consumption

Daily spicy food intake is high (25.6%), despite potential digestive irritation.

15. Fast Food Irritation

32.6% explicitly report that fast food worsens their symptoms.

IV. Clinical Data (16-20)

16. Clinical Diagnosis

While many are undiagnosed, IBS and Colitis affect 14% of the diagnosed group.

17. Stress Levels Assessment

High stress levels are extremely prevalent, affecting 44.2% of participants.

18. Daily Sleep Hours

A majority average 5-7 hours, slightly below clinical sleep recommendations.

19. Regular Exercise Habits

39.5% report no regular exercise, while 53.5% exercise only sometimes.

20. Processed Meat Intake

Daily processed meat consumption is noted in 27.9% of cases, a notable risk factor.
